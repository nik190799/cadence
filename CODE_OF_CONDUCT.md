# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

This project adopts the [Contributor Covenant version 3.0](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/3/0/),
released July 2025, which reframes enforcement around restorative
practice ("Addressing and Repairing Harm").

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation
in Cadence and its community a harassment-free experience for everyone,
regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability,
ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of
experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and
orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open,
welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Encouraged behavior

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our
  mistakes, and learning from the experience
- Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the
  overall community

## Unacceptable behavior

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or
  advances of any kind
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political
  attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email
  address, without their explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces (issues, pull
requests, discussions, chat, conferences, and any other Cadence-related
forum), and also when an individual is officially representing the
community in public spaces.

## Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may
be reported to the project maintainers via:

- Email: cadence-conduct@kadalge.dev
- Or by opening a confidential issue marked `code-of-conduct`

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

## Enforcement — Addressing and Repairing Harm

Following Contributor Covenant 3.0's restorative model, the maintainers
respond to harm with a combination of:

1. **Direct conversation** with the person who caused harm to
   understand context and intent
2. **Acknowledgment of impact** to the person harmed
3. **Concrete commitments** about future behavior (or removal from the
   community if that is the only path to safety)
4. **Follow-up** to verify commitments are kept

The goal is repair and continued participation where safe; removal is a
last resort, not a first one.
